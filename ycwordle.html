Parshat Eikev - Birkat HaMazon

In this week's parsha—Parshat Eikev, the mitzvah of reciting Birkat HaMazon is commanded in the Torah. The passuk says:

"וְאָכַלְתָּ וְשָׂבָעְתָּ, וּבֵרַכְתָּ אֶת-ה' אֱ-לֹקֶיךָ, עַל-הָאָרֶץ הַטּוֹבָה אֲשֶׁר נָתַן-לָּךְ"
"When you have eaten and are satisfied, you shall bless the Lord your God for the good land He has given you."

From this passuk, the Gemara (Berakhot 21a) derives the obligation of Birkat HaMazon as a Torah-level commandment (Mitzvat De'oreita). The rabbis unpack the passuk to underline the order and elements of the blessing: the act of eating and being satisfied, followed by blessing God.

Shabbat Shalom!
